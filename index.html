Welcome to SWAN

Dear Nature and Animal Lover,

Welcome to SWAN (Society for Nature and Animal Welfare)! We are the community of passionate individuals dedicated to making a positive impact on the campus of our university .

At SWAN, we believe that every creature, big or small, deserves our care and compassion. Whether it's advocating for the protection of our planet's diverse ecosystems, promoting the welfare of animals in need, or fostering a deeper connection with nature, each member plays a vital role in our collective mission.

Together, let us embark on this journey of stewardship and advocacy, leaving behind a legacy of love, respect, and preservation for future generations to inherit.

About Us - SWAN

As passionate nature and animal lovers and dedicated students of our university, we, the members of SWAN (Society for Nature and Animal Welfare), are driven by a profound connection to the world around us. For us, it's not merely about existing in this ecosystem; it's about thriving within it, coexisting harmoniously with every living being and element that shares our planet. Our journey is one of empathy, compassion, and advocacy, rooted in the belief that every creature, big or small, plays an integral role in the intricate tapestry of life. With every step we take, every initiative we launch, and every heart we touch, we strive to foster a deeper understanding and appreciation for nature's wonders and the welfare of all its inhabitants. Together, let us embark on this journey of stewardship and care, leaving behind a legacy of love and preservation for generations to come.

Vision

Vidhyadhanam Sarvadhanapradhanam means "Education is the unrivalled treasure of all."
Going along with the university motto, SWAN aims to create a campus where nature and living creatures are cherished and preserved, inspiring a global community passionate about the planet's complex web of life. We aim to create a friendly environment for nature enthusiasts and animal lovers, fostering hope, respect, and responsibility towards the environment. We also strive to promote education and awareness of diverse ecosystems and wildlife.
SWAN is committed to conservation and compassion, focusing on nature safety, flora protection, and equal attention to animals. We aim to provide shelters for animals and provide medical assistance for dogs in India's severe climate change. SWAN also aims for a cleaner environment and encourages volunteers to help workers maintain a hygienic environment. We also focus on identifying green spots and spreading awareness through film screenings, documentaries, photography, and more.

Get Involved

Task Specific Teams

Bark Brigade

Objective: This team will focus on providing care and assistance to our animal friends, particularly dogs.

Responsibilities:

  • Ensuring the well-being of dogs on campus
  • Organizing feeding schedules
  • Arranging medical assistance when needed
  • Fostering a safe and nurturing environment for dogs

Med Heroes

Objective: Comprising medical professionals or volunteers with medical knowledge, this team will cater to the health needs of our furry friends.

Responsibilities:

  • Offering medical assistance to animals in need
  • Providing first aid
  • Coordinating veterinary visits
  • Administering vaccinations and treatments as required

Eco Explorers

Objective: This team, also known as the Nature Buddies, will focus on exploring and preserving the natural environment around us.

Responsibilities:

  • Organizing nature walks
  • Tree planting drives
  • Identifying the spot where we can work towards greenary
  • Raising awareness about the importance of nature

Dirt Busters (Cleanliness Boss)

Objective: Dedicated to maintaining cleanliness on campus, this team will identify areas requiring cleaning and implement strategies to keep our surroundings tidy.

Responsibilities:

  • Conducting cleanliness drives
  • Organizing waste collection and disposal activities
  • Implementing recycling initiatives

Trash Transformers (Waste to Structures)

Objective: Brainstorming innovative ideas to repurpose waste materials into useful structures and items, such as eco-bricks.

Responsibilities:

  • Collaborating on recycling projects
  • Researching and implementing creative solutions for waste management
  • Overseeing the construction of eco-friendly structures on campus

Green Guardians (Awareness Leader)

Objective: Focused on spreading awareness about environmental issues, this team will engage with students and the community through various awareness campaigns.

Responsibilities:

  • Creating informative posters and materials
  • Organizing awareness events and workshops

Data Paws (Survey Animal [Data Superstars])

Objective: Responsible for collecting and maintaining data related to the campus dog population, including health records and behavior observations.

Responsibilities:

  • Conducting surveys
  • Recording dog demographics
  • Tracking vaccination and sterilization records
  • Analyzing data to inform decision-making regarding animal welfare initiatives

Flora Finders (Survey Plants [Data Superstars])

Objective: Tasked with documenting the flora on campus, this team will conduct surveys to identify and catalog plant species and monitor their growth and health.

Responsibilities:

  • Conducting botanical surveys
  • Tagging and labeling plants
  • Creating a plant database
  • Assisting in conservation efforts to protect native vegetation

Mission

"The real wealth of the Nation lies in the resources of the earth - soil, water, forests, minerals, and wildlife." - Rachel Carson.

Firmly believing in the same, our college's Animal and Nature Welfare Society aims to foster a connection between students and the natural world, promoting environmental consciousness through education, community involvement, and sustainable practices, addressing the increasing impact of human activities.
